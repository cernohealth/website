Introduction:
You are being asked to take part in a research study because you have an upcoming consultation at a
sleep clinic. This form gives you information about the study and will help you decide whether you’d like
to participate. Participation in the study is completely voluntary and your decision will not affect the
clinical care you receive at your doctor’s clinic. You are free to discuss this decision with your family and
friends before agreeing to take part in this study. If you have any further questions about the study,
please contact the study staff at the number listed on this form.
Study Background:
Cerno Health has developed software for smartwatches that gather information, or “data,” during sleep.
This software is intended to provide clinicians with additional sleep data and has the potential to allow
for easier long-term tracking and assessments of patients’ sleep conditions and therapy progress. This
study focuses on how accurately Cerno smartwatch applications (on both Apple Watch and Fitbit) can
capture sleep information, when compared to the PSG. It also looks at what at-home sleep data can be
gathered, such as total sleep time and time spent in certain sleep stages during nighttime sleep or
daytime naps.
Study Procedures:
If you choose to participate in this study, you will use your Apple Watch or a Fitbit smartwatch and be
instructed on how to use the device. You will wear the watch for approximately seven days and nights
before your clinic visit. In addition, you will be asked to complete a few questionnaires and keep a sleep
diary for the duration of the trial.
Study Design:
This section of the consent form will detail the schedule of events that will take place if you agree to
participate in the study. Reach out to Cerno if you have questions regarding this schedule.
Day 1: You will receive instructions to set up and use the Apple or Fitbit smartwatch for the study. You will
also fill out a few questionnaires related to your sleep history and current symptoms.
Day 1 - 7: For the next 7 days you will continuously wear the smartwatch during both days and nights,
except when charging the watches.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Why is this study being done?
The purpose of this study is to assess the value of at-home tracking via your smartwatch application to
provide your clinician with valuable information about your sleep. This study will assess important sleep
monitoring metrics such as time spent in certain sleep stages, total sleep time and sleep efficiency.
Where does this study take place?
The study will take place at your home and other locations in your everyday living.
How long will I be in the study?
You will be asked to use the wearables for approximately 7 days and nights of at-home monitoring.
Can I leave the study?
Yes. You can decide to withdraw from the study at any time. Tell the research coordinator or principal
investigator if you are thinking about withdrawing or have decided to withdraw. The research
coordinator will help you finalize your withdrawal from the study.
Additionally, the principal investigator may terminate your participation in the study at any time if they
believe it is in your best interest, if you do not comply with the study guidelines, or if the study is
terminated.
What side effects or risks can I expect from being in the study?
Since this is a non-interventional study, side effects from the study are expected to be none or minimal.
However, potential risks include the smartwatches disrupting your normal sleep, allergic reactions from
wearing the smartwatch bands, and the possible loss of confidentiality of your research-related
information. Speak with study staff if you have any questions or concerns regarding any of these risks.
What benefits can I expect from being in the study?
You may benefit from participating in the study, but this cannot be guaranteed. This trial may uncover
additional insight about your sleep gathered from the wearable devices.
What health information will be collected?
Demographic information including your date of birth, gender, and weight will be collected. Your sleep
related history will be collected. In addition, any information learned during telephone calls,
questionnaires, and data generated from the sleep tests and smartwatches will be collected during the
study. To set up the Apple Watch correctly, you must allow the application access to your personal
Apple Health data.

Who will see and use my health information?
Researchers will collect medical and sleep history, as well as data gathered from the smartwatch
devices and Apple Health to conduct this study. Your data will be de-identified and associated with a
specific subject number so that only those working directly on the study or overseeing the study will
have access to your personally identifying information. In addition, authorized representatives from the
sponsor may review your research data for the purpose of monitoring or managing the conduct of this
study. The de-identified data may be shared with other researchers or analysts or used in future
studies, but this data will not contain any personal or identifying information.
How will information about me be kept confidential?
The loss of confidentiality is a risk associated with most research studies. However, we will take every
effort to ensure your personal information remains confidential. Any personal or identifying information
will not be shared with anyone not directly working on this study. Similarly, any publications or
presentations within the scientific community regarding the data collected in this study will not contain
any identifying information. Still, there is a risk that your information could be seen by others without
your permission. Your personal information may be disclosed if required by law.
Are there any costs to me for taking part in this study?
No.
Will I be paid for taking part in this study?
There is no compensation provided for your participation.
What happens if I get injured from this study?
If you feel you have been injured from taking part in this study, contact the study staff or study doctor to
discuss how to be safely withdrawn from the trial. The risks associated with using Apple or Fitbit watches
for sleep monitoring are deemed minimal, and there are no funds allocated for injury-related
compensation from the sponsor. In the case of injury resulting from this study, you do not lose any of
your legal rights to seek payment by signing this form.
Who can answer my questions about the study?
You can talk to research staff at your doctors clinic about any questions, concerns, or complaints you
have about this study.
